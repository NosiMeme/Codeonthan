Storm Dangers (Expanded)

  • Flooding: Destroys homes, roads, and cars.
  • Power outages: Affect heating, cooling, and medical devices.
  • Road damage: Blocks emergency vehicles.
  • Fires: Caused by lightning or damaged power lines.
  • Contaminated water: Floodwater carries sewage and chemicals.
  • Building collapse: Strong winds weaken structures.
  • Landslides: Heavy rain loosens soil on hills.
  • Air pollution: Wildfire storms create toxic smoke.

Major Storm Events (Expanded)

Hurricane Katrina (2005): Over 1,800 deaths; massive flooding in New Orleans.

Pakistan Floods (2010): Around 1,700 deaths; 20 million people affected.

Hurricane Maria (2017): Thousands of deaths; Puerto Rico lost power for months.

Joplin Tornado (2011): EF5 tornado; 158 deaths and thousands of buildings destroyed.

Typhoon Haiyan (2013): Over 6,000 deaths in the Philippines.

European Heat Wave (2003): Over 70,000 deaths.

Cyclone Idai (2019): Over 1,300 deaths in Africa.

Mental Health Impacts

Storms can cause anxiety, PTSD, fear of future storms, sleep problems, and stress from losing homes or belongings. Children and teens are especially vulnerable.

Environmental Impacts

Storms affect nature too — floods spread pollution, hurricanes destroy beaches, tornadoes uproot forests, and heat waves kill plants and animals.
