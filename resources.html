Storm Safety Resources & Emergency Preparedness

StormGuard gathers essential tools, guides, and information to help you prepare for, respond to, and recover from severe weather events. This includes a step-by-step emergency kit guide, evacuation planning, and trusted resources for families, students, and communities.

1. Step-by-Step Emergency Kit Building Guide

Follow this detailed guide to assemble a storm-ready kit for quick access.

Step 1: Storage & Location

  • Use a waterproof backpack, plastic container, or rolling bin.
  • Keep it near an exit for quick access.
  • Ensure all family members know where it is stored.

Step 2: Water & Food

  • 1 gallon of water per person per day (minimum 3 days).
  • Non-perishable food: canned goods, protein bars, dry cereal, peanut butter.
  • Manual can opener and disposable utensils.
  • Special items for infants, elderly, or pets (food, formula, water).

Step 3: Medical Supplies

  • First aid kit (bandages, antiseptic wipes, gloves, gauze).
  • Prescription medications (at least 3–7 day supply).
  • Pain relievers, fever reducers, thermometer.
  • Hand sanitizer, disinfectant wipes, masks.

Step 4: Lighting & Communication

  • Flashlights for each family member + extra batteries.
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio for weather updates.
  • Portable chargers/power banks for phones.
  • Whistle to signal for help.

Step 5: Documents & Financial Items

  • Copies of ID, birth certificates, insurance policies, medical records.
  • Cash in small bills and backup credit cards.
  • Store documents in waterproof, sealed bags.

Step 6: Clothing & Personal Protection

  • Change of clothes, sturdy shoes/boots, rain jackets.
  • Blankets or sleeping bags for each family member.
  • Work gloves for debris cleanup.

Step 7: Safety Tools

  • Multi-tool or basic tool kit.
  • Plastic sheeting & duct tape for temporary repairs.
  • Fire extinguisher and local area maps.

2. Evacuation Planning Worksheet

Being prepared includes a clear evacuation plan. Fill out this worksheet for your household.

  • Primary evacuation route: __________________________
  • Secondary backup route: __________________________
  • Nearest emergency shelter: __________________________
  • Out-of-town emergency contact: __________________________
  • Meeting location outside neighborhood: __________________________
  • Pet evacuation plan: Carrier, leash, food, vaccination records.

Quick Access Tips

  • Review and update your kit every 6 months.
  • Replace expired food, water, and medications.
  • Keep shoes and flashlight next to each bed.
  • Keep car gas tank at least half full during storm season.
  • Practice evacuation drills with your family twice per year.

3. Trusted Online Resources

Use these official sources to stay informed and prepared:

  • FEMA (fema.gov): Disaster planning, checklists, and recovery guidance.
  • Ready.gov: Emergency kit guides, evacuation worksheets, and family plans.
  • CDC Emergency Preparedness: Health and medical guidance for storms.
  • National Weather Service (weather.gov): Alerts, watches, warnings, radar maps.
  • American Red Cross (redcross.org): Shelter information, blood donations, and community recovery resources.

4. Community Support & Recovery

  • Local shelters, food banks, and volunteer organizations.
  • Emergency hotlines and crisis counseling services.
  • Support for pets, elderly, disabled, and medically vulnerable residents.
  • Insurance and recovery claim guidance.
  • Mold prevention and safe cleanup instructions.

By combining a well-prepared emergency kit, a clear evacuation plan, and access to trusted community and online resources, you dramatically increase your safety and the safety of your loved ones during storms.
